Lesson 2: Online Privacy & Digital Footprint

Introduction

Everything you do online leaves a trail called your digital footprint. From social media posts to browsing habits, companies and hackers alike can learn a lot about you. Protecting your online privacy helps you stay safe from identity theft, scams, and unwanted tracking.

Key Concepts

Digital Footprint: The information you leave behind online, intentionally or unintentionally.

Tracking: Websites and advertisers collect your browsing behavior for ads or profiling.

Privacy Settings: Tools that let you control what others see about you.

Real-Life Scenario

A student posts a “funny” picture online. Years later, a job recruiter finds it and questions their professionalism. This shows how digital footprints can affect the future.

Best Practices

  • Think before sharing personal info on social media.
  • Check and update your privacy settings regularly.
  • Use private browsing modes or VPNs for sensitive tasks.
  • Avoid oversharing personal details in online communities.
