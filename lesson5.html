Lesson 5: Data Protection & Encryption

Introduction

Our digital world runs on data — from private messages to bank transactions. Protecting this data ensures safety and privacy. One of the strongest tools in cybersecurity is encryption, which scrambles data so only the right people can read it.

Key Concepts

  • Encryption: The process of converting information into unreadable code.
  • Decryption: Turning encrypted data back into readable information with a key.
  • Backup: Making copies of data to protect against loss or corruption.
  • Data Breach: When unauthorized people access sensitive data.

Everyday Examples

Messaging apps like WhatsApp and Signal use end-to-end encryption to keep conversations private, so even the service providers can’t read them.

Best Practices for Data Protection

  • Use strong, unique passwords for accounts.
  • Enable encryption on devices and cloud storage.
  • Keep regular backups of important data.
  • Be cautious when sharing files online.
